This member is not due a Remediable Service Statement (RSS) yet

This member is affected by the McCloud remedy, but is not due their RSS yet. This could be because we're not paying their pension benefits yet.

Their annual benefit statement (ABS) will show them what their retirement options might look like depending on which remedy choice they make when they retire.

On retirement, some members may need their RSS sooner if they meet certain criteria. You can read more about this in our When you'll make your remedy choice (opens in new tab) page.

If the member's payments have already started

If their payments started recently

We may not have a delivery date yet for a member's RSS if their pension payments have only started recently. You can check again another day or contact us.

If they partially retired before 31 March 2015

If the member partially retired before 31 March 2015 and has only claimed 1995 Section benefits, the pension payments they're receiving now are not affected by the McCloud remedy.

Their remedy choice is between 2008 Scheme and 2015 Scheme benefits, which are not currently being paid.

If they have decoupled 1995 benefits

If the member retired and only claimed decoupled 1995 Scheme benefits, the pension payments they're receiving now are not affected by the McCloud Remedy.

This is because they left the NHS Pension Scheme for over 5 years before re-joining, meaning their 1995 Section benefits stayed separated from their new membership (also known as ‘decoupled’).

Their remedy choice is between 2008 Scheme and 2015 Scheme benefits, which are not currently being paid.
